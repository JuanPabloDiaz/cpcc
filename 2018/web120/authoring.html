3). Identify and explain the four basic navigation structures.
a) Linear - means the screens are viewed one after the other, much like the pages of a book. also called sequential because of it simplicity and easy to follow one after the other.
b) Hierarchical - means that the navigation is like a filter where it starts at the top with the most generic topics and while scrolling down, it filters down to more specific options. like grouping.
c) Networked - means that users are able to explore more freely by replacing sequences or hierarchies with a variety of user options. A common example will be hyperlinks.
d) Conditional - means that access to information is contingent on the user’s responses or progress in the application.
